\title{Immersed boundary simulations of cell-cell interactions in whole blood}

\author[1]{Andrew Kassen\corref{corr}}  \ead{kassen@math.utah.edu}
\author[1]{Aaron Barrett}               \ead{barrett@math.utah.edu}
\author[2]{Varun Shankar}               \ead{shankar@cs.utah.edu}
\author[1]{Aaron L. Fogelson\fnref{3}}  \ead{fogelson@math.utah.edu}

\cortext[corr]{Corresponding author}
\address[1]{Department of Mathematics, University of Utah, Salt Lake City, UT 84112, USA}
\address[2]{School of Computing, University of Utah, Salt Lake City, UT 84112, USA}
\address[3]{Department of Bioengineering, University of Utah, Salt Lake City, UT 84112, USA}


\begin{abstract}
    We use radial basis functions to represent cells in immersed boundary simulations of
    whole blood, focusing on the interaction between red blood cells, platelets, and the
    endothelium. We consider the effect of endothelial geometry on platelet motion. We
    find red blood cells to be crucial for understanding the motion of platelets, to the
    point that wall geometry has a negligible effect. We observe behaviors for which the
    platelet remains near the endothelium for extended periods and red blood
    cell-mediated interactions between platelets and endothelium for which the platelet
    has reduced speed. We suggest that these behaviors as mechanisms for vascular
    maintenance.
\end{abstract}
